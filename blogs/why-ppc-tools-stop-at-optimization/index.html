The Line Every Tool Stops At

In traditional advertising, this problem was solved decades ago. TV campaigns measure incremental lift through geo-based holdout tests: "$2.8M incremental revenue (±$200K, 95% confidence)." Display advertising tracks view-through attribution. Paid search separates branded protection from net new acquisition.

Every channel evolved to answer one fundamental question: Compared to what?

For mid-market Amazon sellers, PPC often represents 40-50% of total revenue—the single largest marketing budget line and most controllable growth lever. Yet it gets measured with less rigor than channels representing a fraction of traditional brand budgets.

The question isn't "how does PPC attribution compare to TV attribution?" The question is: Why does your largest revenue driver have the least measurement rigor?

The answer reveals why the entire category stops at the same line.
